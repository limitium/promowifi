Altai-Koks

Location: Zarinsk, Altai Territory

Processing capacity: 4.4 million tonnes of coke a year, dry weight


Altai-Koks is one of Russia’s largest coke plants. It produces 14% of all Russian coke. NLMK Group’s total share of the Russian market is 22%.

The company is located in the Siberian Federal District, close to the mining companies of Kuznetsk Coal Basin, where 80% of all Russia’s coke is mined.

Altai-Koks produces high-quality coke and chemical products using advanced production technologies. For example, in 2013, thanks to their joint work in creating a technological union, Novolipetsk and Altai-Koks produced the highest-quality coke in Russia, increasing its CSR to 60%–64% and reducing its reactivity index to 25–28%. This allowed the company to move away from importing coal and return to the export markets, stabilize blast-furnace production at Novolipetsk, and increase primary processing efficiency.

Output

Altai-Koks produces around 4 million tonnes of coke per year, dry weight. Most of its production is sent to the Lipetsk plant. Other consumers are Russian, CIS, European, and Asian companies.

The main output is produced on five coke batteries. The launch of the fifth coke battery in 2006 gave the company the capacity to produce 80 mm-grade foundry coke. This fraction is in high demand on the market and is used in the production of high-tech thermal insulating materials.

The company also produces a wide range of high-quality chemical products. By-products of coke production include various chemical materials including coal tar, benzene, and ammonium sulphate.
