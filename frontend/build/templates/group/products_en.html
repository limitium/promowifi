Slabs

Thickness: 180 — 355mm
Width: 0.75 — 2.25m
Length: 6 — 12m

Usage

NLMK produces steel slabs with various chemical properties and in different sizes. The company uses slabs mainly in flat steel production at the Lipetsk plant, and also supplies them to the Group’s mills in Europe and the USA, as well as to other consumers in Russia and abroad. ‘Wide’ slabs are further converted into thick plates, including at NLMK’s subsidiaries in Europe – DanSteel and Clabecq.

Today, NLMK offers Russian consumers unique metal goods which previously had to be imported into the country. For example, as part of its strategic partnership with OMK Group, Novolipetsk manufactures slabs for the production of large-diameter pipes. In a record space of time, specialists at the plant managed to set up production of steel slabs with more rigorous demands on the chemical content and macrostructure of the metal, in standard sizes that were new to Russia.

As a result, a completely Russian high-tech production chain was created as a substitute for imports, dealing with everything from mining and smelting to the manufacture of products important to the country. In less than two years of collaboration, Novolipetsk supplied OMK with over one million tonnes of steel slabs for the manufacture of large diameter pipes used in projects such as South Stream, Nord Stream, and Power of Siberia.

Hot-rolled flat steel

Width: up to 1850mm
Thickness: 1.5 — 16mm

Usage

Hot-rolled flat steel is used in the production of cold-rolled coils and other high value added products at the Lipetsk plant and at VIZ-Steel. Hot-rolled steel is mainly used in the production of steel structures, guardrails, ship hulls, machine casings, road-building machinery components, pressure vessels, and building structures.

Cold-rolled flat steel

Width: up to 1820mm
Thickness: 0.35 — 2.5mm

Usage

NLMK occupies leading positions in the production of cold-rolled steel, with a 24% share of the Russian market. NLMK’s cold-rolled flat steel is used to produce pre-painted and electrical steel at the Lipetsk plant, and is also sold to other companies in Russia and abroad. It is widely used in the production of body parts for machines and equipment, load-bearing structures, pipes, lighting masts, and agricultural equipment.

Thick plate

Width: up to 3100mm
Thickness: up to 50mm

Usage

The company produces a wide range of thick plates, including special types and grades. NLMK’s premium steel products are Quard, a thick, low-alloy, hard-wearing steel plate and Quend, a high-duty construction steel.

The main consumers are power and mechanical-engineering companies, including those in the heavy engineering, shipbuilding and pipe manufacturing sectors, in European and global markets.

Abrasion-resistant Quard steel is used in the production of construction and mining equipment and other types of special equipment and machinery. Using this grade of steel, the weight of a construction element is reduced by 40% with a 5% increase in its volume; fuel consumption is lowered and production costs fall by 45%.

High-strength Quend steel is used in the manufacture of lorry undercarriages, hoisting machinery in the construction industry and other equipment where high durability is vital. Using Quend steel sheets, the weight of structural components is reduced by more than 20%, thus reducing production costs and increasing performance.

Galvanized steel

Width: up to 1800mm
Thickness: 0.22 — 3.0mm

Usage

NLMK is one of the largest manufacturers of galvanized steel in Russia, with a 17% share of the market. Galvanized steel is used in the production of machine body parts, roofing materials, load-bearing structures in hostile environments, car parts and components of household electrical devices. NLMK also manufactures titanium-alloy steel with an ultra-low carbon content for extra deep drawing (IF steel), which increases the plasticity of galvanized steel.

Width of steel: 900 — 1800mm

Coil mass: 5 — 15tonnes
Mass of plate batch: up to 10tonnes

Usage

NLMK is the largest manufacturer of pre-painted steel in Russia, with a 21% share of the market. Modern equipment and advanced technology guarantee high-quality pre-painted steel. This type of steel boasts high resistance to atmospheric corrosion, has decorative qualities, and combines strength and plasticity.

Usage

NLMK is one of the leading manufacturers of electrical steel, occupying around 85% of the Russian market for this high-tech product. Rebar

Grade: А500С
Diameter: 12 — 25mm

Usage

NLMK manufactures rebar at NLMK Kaluga, located close to Moscow – the largest construction market in Russia. The bulk of sales are to the domestic market. Rebar is used mainly to reinforce concrete structures.

Metalware

885 different types and sizes of metalware

Usage

NLMK Metalware produces a wide range of metalware, from construction nails to wire for reinforcing concrete structures. It manufactures 885 different types and sizes of metalware. Its products are used primarily in the construction industry.

The product with the highest demand is wire rod for reinforcing concrete structures, galvanized wire, welding wire, construction nails and fixing hardware. The company exports its products to 18 countries worldwide.

Iron ore concentrate

Iron content: 66.5%

Usage

Stoilensky produces iron ore concentrate (with an average iron content of 66.5%) and sinter ore (52%), which is used as a raw material in steelworks.

Stoilensky’s production fully meets NLMK Group’s demand for iron ore concentrate. Concentrate reserves are estimated at 5 billion tonnes, meaning the Group’s current output can be sustained for over 180 years. The concentrate has one of the lowest production costs in the industry, amounting to an average of USD 20 per tonne.

Fluxing limestone

Usage

Fluxing limestone is used as an addition agent in furnace burdens, in pellets or sinter.

Carbonates are used in the production of both limestone used to manufacture construction lime and limestone used as a fluxing material in steelmaking. The stone is mined in an open pit and includes the use of explosives.

Supplies from Stagdok and Dolomit fully cover NLMK Group’s requirements for the main types of flux.

Coke

Usage

Altai-Koks produces around 4 million tonnes of coke per year, dry weight. Most of its production is sent to the Lipetsk plant. Other consumers are Russian, CIS, European, and Asian companies.

The main output is produced on five coke batteries. The launch of the fifth coke battery in 2006 gave the company the capacity to produce 80 mm-grade foundry coke. This fraction is in high demand on the market and is used in the production of high-tech thermal insulating materials.

The company also produces a wide range of high-quality chemical products. By-products of coke production include various chemical materials including coal tar, benzene, and ammonium sulphate.
